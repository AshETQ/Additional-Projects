# MiniBobCAT Concept

I created MiniBobcat as a prototype for an "under 1 kg"/half scaled version of the multi-modal BoBCAT platform from FSU's STRIDe Lab. I designed the C-shaped foot for this robot with the goal of tri-modality. MBC was able to walk, swim, and was able to hold its own weight on the wall, but testing was not achieved. The robot had custom boards soldered and the code was run through Arduino/Teensyduino. Unfortunately, the platform gave me a lot of knowledge on water proofing do's and don'ts and was retired.

# PropBoB 

PropBoB was an experimental design to add a propellor onto the <a href="https://bigidea.nianet.org/past-competition-themes/2022-forum-results/" target="_blank">**BoB Family** </a> of climbing robots, with the goal to drive the robot further into the wall for stabilization. This work was not published, but results were used to aide student's research.

# Optimizing Dynamic Legged Locomotion in Mixed, Resistive Media

Paper <a href="https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9863276/" target="_blank">**here** </a>.

I assisted in the testing and validation of this paper through running a five-bar linkage through a simulated fluid bed boom arm. I repeated experiments to conduct a Nelder-Mead Optimization, working in collaboration with the Army Research Laboratory.

# The DAVEs - Dynamics and Vibrations Equipment for lab learning

The DAVE platforms were created in collaboration with another graduate student and TA to assist the studetns of Systems Vibrations in learning modules. I wrote up instructions, guided problems, and questions for the students in lab instructions/reports for each of the thirteen weeks and problem sets. I assisted in modifying the DAVE stations each week to prepare for each learning module, and helped debug code (arudino, MATLAB) issues. I also soldered components on the custom, CNC created PCBs for each station. We created and maintained over 20 lab stations, and oversaw the students use of them, helping as needed.

# Senior Design- Creating a Flax Fiber Composite

My senior design team researched and worked to physically create a flax fiber based composite with eco-friendly resin for Sikorsky Aircraft. Our sponsor tasked us to characterize the composite to explore its possible use on an aircraft. My team created multiple samples and characterized each through tensile, compression, vibration, and sheer testing. We won second place in our senior design day poster presentation expo!
